ANCHOR CABLE & MOORING LINE INSPECTION GUIDE
=============================================
Ref: ACI-2023-R2

This guide applies to all catenary and taut mooring systems at Maithon floating solar.

1. Visual Inspection (Weekly)
   - Look for frayed strands, kinks, or bird damage on polyester mooring ropes.
   - Check for excessive marine growth that adds weight.
   - Inspect thimbles and spliced eyes for deformation.

2. Mechanical Testing (Bi-Annual)
   - Perform tension test using calibrated load cell.
   - Compare measured tension with design tension. Acceptable deviation: ±10%.
   - If tension is out of range, adjust by rotating turnbuckle.

3. Corrosion Assessment of Steel Components
   - Anchor chains, shackles, and tensioning hardware shall be inspected for rust.
   - Apply marine-grade anti-corrosion paint if surface rust is observed.
   - Replace any component with section loss >5%.

4. Anchor Integrity
   - For gravity anchors, inspect concrete surface for cracks.
   - For helical anchors, verify no vertical movement.
   - Record water depth at anchor location; if depth has changed by >1m, re-evaluate anchor holding capacity.

5. Emergency Procedures
   - If a mooring line breaks, immediately isolate the affected floating island by disconnecting walkways.
   - Notify O&M Manager and deploy standby anchor system.
