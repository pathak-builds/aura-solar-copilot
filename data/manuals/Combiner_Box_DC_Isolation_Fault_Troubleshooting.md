# DC Isolation Fault – Combiner Box Level Troubleshooting

## 1. Introduction
DC isolation faults are one of the most common faults at Maithon floating solar plant due to high humidity and occasional submersion of combiner boxes during monsoon.

## 2. Safety Precautions
- Lock Out Tag Out (LOTO) on all DC inputs from strings.
- Confirm zero voltage with CAT IV multimeter.
- Wear rubber insulating gloves and dielectric overshoes.

## 3. Step-by-Step Isolation Procedure
1. Open combiner box door and visually inspect for water or condensation.
2. If moisture present, dry using industrial hot air blower before proceeding.
3. Disconnect each fuse holder and megger test individual strings:
   - Positive to ground
   - Negative to ground
   - Resistance must be >1 MOhm.
4. If a string shows low insulation, follow that string’s cable route over the floating platform.
5. Look for:
   - Cable ties cutting into cable insulation due to platform movement.
   - Rodent damage (especially near anchor points).
   - Submerged MC4 connectors (common after heavy rain).
6. Repair: Replace damaged cable section, install IP68 rated junction box if necessary.

## 4. Preventative Measures
- Replace all cable glands with double-compression type.
- Apply hydrophobic coating inside combiner box.
- Install water level sensor in combiner box (optional).
